# Polarized Training Model — TrainingCoach (2026)

## Overview

Polarized training is an intensity distribution model where the majority of training volume (~80%) is performed at low intensity and the remainder (~20%) at high intensity, with minimal time spent at moderate/threshold intensity. This model is supported by observational studies of elite endurance athletes and controlled intervention trials.

## The Seiler Model (2010)

Stephen Seiler's landmark 2010 review synthesized decades of research on elite endurance athletes across rowing, cycling, running, cross-country skiing, and swimming. The key finding: successful endurance athletes across sports converge on a similar intensity distribution.

### The 80/20 Distribution

```
~80% of sessions at low intensity (Zone 1 — below first ventilatory threshold)
~0-5% of sessions at moderate intensity (Zone 2 — between VT1 and VT2)
~15-20% of sessions at high intensity (Zone 3 — above second ventilatory threshold)
```

Seiler uses a **3-zone model** based on ventilatory/lactate thresholds:

| Seiler Zone | Physiological Boundary | Perceived Effort | Blood Lactate |
|-------------|----------------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Zone 1 | Below VT1 / LT1 | Conversational, easy | < 2 mmol/L |
| Zone 2 | Between VT1 and VT2 | Comfortably hard, "tempo" | 2 - 4 mmol/L |
| Zone 3 | Above VT2 / LT2 | Hard to maximal | > 4 mmol/L |

### Why Polarized Works

1. **High volume at low intensity** builds aerobic base, mitochondrial density, capillarization, and fat oxidation capacity without accumulating excessive fatigue
2. **High-intensity sessions** provide the stimulus for VO2max improvement, lactate clearance, and neuromuscular adaptations
3. **Avoiding the moderate zone** ("black hole training") prevents chronic moderate fatigue that impairs recovery without providing sufficient high-intensity stimulus
4. The moderate zone produces the worst cost:benefit ratio — significant fatigue for modest adaptation

## Stoggl & Sperlich (2014) — Intervention Trial

Stoggl and Sperlich (2014) conducted the first controlled study directly comparing four training intensity distributions in trained endurance athletes over 9 weeks:

| Group | Distribution | Key Outcome |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| High Volume (HVT) | 83% low / 17% moderate | Moderate VO2max improvement |
| Threshold (THR) | 46% low / 54% moderate | Least improvement |
| High Intensity (HIT) | 43% low / 57% high | Good improvement but high fatigue |
| **Polarized (POL)** | **68% low / 6% moderate / 26% high** | **Greatest VO2max, time to exhaustion, body composition improvements** |

Key findings:
- Polarized produced the largest improvements in VO2max (+6.8%), time to exhaustion, body composition, and peak power/velocity
- Threshold training was the least effective despite being the most popular approach among recreational athletes
- Polarized athletes reported lower subjective fatigue despite superior outcomes

## Per-Sport Intensity Distribution

### Cycling — 7-Zone to 3-Zone Mapping

| Coggan Zone | Seiler Zone | Classification |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| Zone 1 (Active Recovery) | Zone 1 | LOW |
| Zone 2 (Endurance) | Zone 1 | LOW |
| Zone 3 (Tempo) | Zone 2 | MODERATE |
| Zone 4 (Threshold) | Zone 2-3 boundary | MODERATE / HIGH |
| Zone 5 (VO2max) | Zone 3 | HIGH |
| Zone 6 (Anaerobic) | Zone 3 | HIGH |
| Zone 7 (Neuromuscular) | Zone 3 | HIGH |

Polarized cycling target: ~80% of time in Coggan Zones 1-2, ~20% in Zones 5-7, minimal Zone 3-4.

### Running — 6-Zone to 3-Zone Mapping

| Running Zone | Seiler Zone | Classification |
|-------------|-------------|----------------|

| Zone 2 (Aerobic) | Zone 1 | LOW |
| Zone 3 (Tempo) | Zone 2 | MODERATE |
| Zone 4 (Threshold) | Zone 2-3 boundary | MODERATE / HIGH |
| Zone 5 (VO2max) | Zone 3 | HIGH |
| Zone 6 (Anaerobic) | Zone 3 | HIGH |

Polarized running target: ~80% of time in Zones 1-2, ~20% in Zones 5-6, minimal Zone 3-4.

### Swimming — 5-Zone to 3-Zone Mapping

| Swim Zone | Seiler Zone | Classification |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| Zone 1 (Recovery) | Zone 1 | LOW |
| Zone 2 (Endurance) | Zone 1 | LOW |
| Zone 3 (Tempo) | Zone 2 | MODERATE |
| Zone 4 (Threshold / CSS) | Zone 2-3 boundary | MODERATE / HIGH |
| Zone 5 (VO2max / Speed) | Zone 3 | HIGH |

Polarized swimming target: ~80% of time in Zones 1-2, ~20% in Zone 5, minimal Zone 3-4.

**Note**: Swimming is inherently more technique-dependent. Low-intensity swimming still requires attention to stroke mechanics, making "easy" swim sessions actively productive beyond pure aerobic development.

## Intensity Distribution Targets

### By Training Phase

| Phase | Low (%) | Moderate (%) | High (%) | Notes |
|-------|---------|-------------|----------|-------|
| Base / general prep | 80 - 85 | 5 - 10 | 10 - 15 | Build aerobic foundation |
| Build / specific prep | 75 - 80 | 5 - 10 | 15 - 20 | Increase high-intensity stimulus |
| Peak / competition | 75 - 80 | 0 - 5 | 20 - 25 | Sharpen with quality, reduce volume |
| Recovery / transition | 90 - 100 | 0 - 5 | 0 - 5 | Active recovery only |

### Session Count vs Time Distribution

The 80/20 split can be measured by:
- **Session count**: ~4 easy sessions per 1 hard session
- **Time in zone**: ~80% of total minutes at low intensity
- **TSS contribution**: High-intensity sessions contribute disproportionate TSS despite less time

TrainingCoach tracks time-in-zone distribution and alerts athletes if moderate intensity creeps above 10% of weekly volume (the "black hole" warning).

## Practical Implementation

### Weekly Pattern Examples

**5 sessions/week (single sport)**:
- 4 easy sessions (Mon, Wed, Thu, Sat) — Zone 1-2
- 1 hard session (Tue) — Zone 3+ intervals
- Ratio: 80% easy / 20% hard

**8 sessions/week (triathlon)**:
- 6 easy sessions across sports — Zone 1-2
- 2 hard sessions (1 bike intervals, 1 run intervals) — Zone 3+
- Ratio: 75% easy / 25% hard

### Common Mistakes

1. **Too much moderate intensity** — recreational athletes default to "comfortably hard" every session
2. **Easy sessions not easy enough** — Zone 1-2 should be truly conversational
3. **Hard sessions not hard enough** — if you do fewer hard sessions, make them count
4. **Ignoring session context** — a hard group ride that wasn't planned still counts as high intensity

## Recent Research Updates (2020-2025)

- Rosenblat et al. (2022): Meta-analysis confirmed polarized training produces greater VO2max improvements than threshold training in trained athletes
- Burnley & Jones (2021): Refined the critical power model showing that work above critical power (analogous to Zone 3) depletes W' reserves exponentially
- Kenneally et al. (2020): Systematic review found elite runners predominantly train with polarized or pyramidal distribution
- Hydren & Cohen (2021): Confirmed that session RPE can reliably classify training intensity for polarized distribution monitoring
